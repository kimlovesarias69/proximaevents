EROS ASCENDS

The Program Performer Bios Questions

Born in São Paulo, Brazil, Gleice de Oliveira Uzur began her violin studies at age five and earned her BM from Escola Municipal de Música Santa Marcelina. Her musical journey includes postgraduate studies in Sofia, Bulgaria, summer festivals in Austria and Germany, and studies with Professor Peter Zazofsky at Boston University.

As the recipient of the prestigious Brazilian Ministry of Culture award, Gleice was the only South American selected for the Australian String Academy in 2009. She has served as Concertmaster and Associate Concertmaster of Orchestra Bachiana, São Paulo Symphony Youth Orchestra, and Orquestrade Câmara da Universidade de São Paulo.

Gleice has also performed extensively with chamber ensembles such as the FASM String Quartet, Ethos String Quartet, Candle Trio, and OCAM String Quartet. As an educator, she taught at the São Paulo Young Philharmonic Orchestra and Guri Santa Marcelina, a social program for at-risk students in São Paulo.

Currently, she is an adjunct violin and music theory instructor at Weber State University.

Raised in Japan, 郁金香 (Jin-Xiang Yu) is a soprano/mezzo-soprano with roots from China, Japan, Korea, and Russia. She grew up in a family of musicians and painters, speaking Japanese and Mandarin Chinese while learning English and Spanish at international schools. Her artistic journey started with ballet, piano, and jazz trumpet, and her commitment to vocal studies developed during her collegiate studies in linguistics.

When not on stage, she works as a film/commercial actor, teaches at her private studio, Studio JX, teaches musical theatre @utahdanceinstitute and is an owner of @yogasunne. JX holds a Musical Theatre degree (American Musical and Dramatic Academy), a Bachelor of Arts in Linguistics (CUNY Queens College), a Bachelor of Music (Aaron Copland School of Music), and a Master of Music (Yale School of Music).

At home, she's a mom to 3 munchkin performers and a wife to Bill, the extraordinaire behind the scenes. She is ridiculously competitive at volleyball, eats unreasonably spicy food, and is indubitably passionate about authentic storytelling. jinxiangyusoprano.com · @jin.xiang_yu / @studio.j_x

A "fluid" musician (Utah Arts Review), Nicholas Maughan is a Salt Lake City–based pianist whose work centers on collaboration, responsiveness, and shared artistic practice. Whether performing in concert settings, working in the dance studio, or collaborating with singers and instrumentalists, Nic approaches music-making as a dialogue shaped by listening, adaptability, and connection.

It was a joy for Nic to focus deeply on his work with Ballet West for six seasons, where he served as Company Pianist and Principal Keyboard with the Ballet West Orchestra. During this time, he supported daily classes, rehearsals, performances, and the creation of new choreographic works, experiences that continue to shape his musical instincts and collaborative approach. Nic has been energized by a recent return to teaching and freelance performance, reconnecting with a wide range of artists and projects across disciplines.

Nic is a founding director of Proxima Performing Arts, which he co-founded with soprano Kimberly Waite and arts advocate Barbara Liau. Through Proxima, he curates and presents cross-disciplinary performances in homes and alternative venues, bringing artists and audiences into close proximity through shared music, food, and conversation. The work reflects a belief that performance is most alive when it is experienced directly, where artists and audiences share the same ground and where connection extends beyond the performance into community.

This past spring, Nic was pleased to return to Weber State University as an adjunct instructor in the dance program, where he teaches "Music for Dancers." His teaching draws directly from professional experience and emphasizes clear communication and collaborative music-making between dancers and musicians.

Kimberly Waite is a vocalist, teacher, and cultural leader rooted in the belief that art and community are inseparable. She sang professionally until 2017, after which she shifted her focus to teaching voice full time. Over the following years, she worked with hundreds of students, helping them reconnect with their voices as instruments of expression, presence, and truth.

In 2023, she closed her private voice studio to redirect her energy toward building institutions designed to serve communities in a rapidly changing era. She is the co-founder and CEO of Opus, a tech company building the next generation of self-discovery tools, and a co-founder and Executive Director of Proxima Performing Arts, a nonprofit devoted to creating intimate, human-centered performance experiences.

Her work is guided by a vision of building organizations that meet the real needs of the Utah Tribe—places that sustain joy, cultivate meaning, and keep love alive in the hearts of everyone who encounters them. Kim is the mother of two brilliant children and is deeply devoted to mysticism, practical spirituality, and her partner, Christopher.

Jared Gilmore is a pianist and composer based in Utah. He studied classical piano through age 18, then stepped away from formal training to explore blues and rock. After that, he moved to New York City to work as an audio engineer and producer, which later led to production work on cruise ship music festivals—where he'd spend off-hours writing on the practice pianos tucked away on the ship.

Today, Jared performs and composes original romantic-style piano music. His writing is influenced by myth, dreams, and depth psychology, and composing is one of the main ways he stays connected to his inner life and the meanings he's tracking beneath the surface.

Barbara Liau is a founder and technology executive with a background in blockchain and distributed systems. She has founded and led multiple venture-backed companies, including Summa and Nomad, where she built infrastructure for connecting and securing digital assets across networks and raised significant institutional capital to support that work.

Her background spans software engineering, product design, and communications, with earlier experience as a developer at Storj and in public affairs with the United States military.

She has also worked as a mentor and educator, supporting the development of early-career engineers.

Barbara currently serves as Board President of Proxima Performing Arts and as CTO of Opus, where she focuses on building practical systems at the intersection of technology and human experience.
